# Data Sources

## Victoria Free Public Transport Experiment 2026

This project combines multiple independent data sources to evaluate the impacts of Victoria's 2026 public transport fare initiatives, including the free public transport period (31 March to 31 May 2026) and the subsequent 50% fare reduction period.

The use of multiple data sources allows behavioural, traffic and operational outcomes to be examined from different perspectives.

---

## 1. Victorian Travel Behaviour Survey

### Description

A statewide survey of Victorian residents undertaken during May 2026 to understand behavioural responses to the free public transport initiative.

### Sample

* Final analytic sample: 1,028 Victorian residents
* Geographic coverage: Metropolitan Melbourne and regional Victoria
* Survey mode: Online questionnaire
* Topics covered:

  * Travel behaviour before and during the policy period
  * Public transport use
  * Car use and mode substitution
  * Future travel intentions
  * Public attitudes towards transport affordability measures
  * Open-text comments and qualitative feedback

### Purpose

The survey provides insights into how people responded to the fare initiative and why travel behaviour changed.

---

## 2. SCATS Traffic Data

### Description

Traffic volume data collected from the Sydney Coordinated Adaptive Traffic System (SCATS) operated on major arterial roads throughout Melbourne.

### Corridors Analysed

* Eastern Freeway corridor
* Nepean Highway corridor

### Metrics

* Daily traffic volumes
* Weekday and weekend comparisons
* Period-of-day analysis
* Baseline versus policy-period comparisons

### Purpose

SCATS data provides objective evidence of changes in road traffic conditions during the fare intervention period.

---

## 3. TIRTL Traffic Data

### Description

Traffic monitoring data collected from TIRTL vehicle counting stations on major Melbourne transport corridors.

### Corridors Analysed

* Monash Freeway corridor
* Tullamarine Freeway corridor
* West Gate corridor

### Metrics

* Daily traffic volumes
* Weekday and weekend comparisons
* Period-of-day analysis
* Baseline versus policy-period comparisons

### Purpose

TIRTL data complements SCATS observations and provides additional coverage of Melbourne's strategic road network.

---

## 4. GTFS Public Transport Data

### Description

General Transit Feed Specification (GTFS) data published through the Victorian public transport open data program.

### Metrics

* Scheduled services
* Route information
* Stop information
* Service frequency
* Network structure

### Purpose

GTFS data provides information about public transport supply and network operations during the study period.

## 5. Public Transport Patronage Data (Proposed)

### Status

Under discussion with the Victorian Department of Transport and Planning.

### Potential Scope

* Daily train station patronage
* Daily train line patronage
* Daily tram route patronage
* Daily bus route patronage
* Network-level patronage indicators

### Purpose

Patronage data would allow behavioural and traffic changes to be compared directly with public transport demand.

---

## 6. DSpark Mobility Data (Under Review)

### Status

Application currently under review through AURIN.

### Potential Scope

Aggregated and privacy-preserving mobility indicators, including:

* Origin-destination movements
* Temporal travel patterns
* Travel distance indicators
* Mobility trends during the fare intervention period

### Purpose

DSpark data would provide a broader understanding of mobility patterns across Victoria and support analysis of travel behaviour beyond individual transport modes.

---

## Data Integration Approach

The project adopts a multi-source evidence framework.

Survey data provides behavioural insights and user perspectives.

Traffic monitoring data provides objective measurements of changes in road use.

Public transport datasets provide operational and demand-side evidence.

Together, these datasets support a comprehensive evaluation of Victoria's 2026 public transport fare initiatives.

---

## Data Availability

This repository contains aggregated results and visualisations only.

Raw survey responses, traffic monitoring datasets and other restricted data sources are not publicly released.

All published outputs comply with research ethics requirements, privacy obligations and applicable data-sharing agreements.
