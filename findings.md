# Findings

## June 2026, Version 1.0

## Behavioural findings

During the free public transport period:

| Indicator | Result |
|---|---:|
| Increased public transport use | 39.5% |
| Reduced car use | 28.8% |
| Replaced some car trips with public transport | 38.2% |
| Increased overall travel | 16.1% |
| Supported affordable public transport during economic pressure | 77.4% |

The findings suggest the policy encouraged many Victorians to experiment with public transport.

Free fares were an important factor, but respondents also identified fuel prices, convenience, work arrangements and personal circumstances as influences on travel behaviour.

## Traffic findings

| Corridor | Average traffic change |
|---|---:|
| Tullamarine | -6.4% |
| Monash | -3.2% |
| Eastern | -2.2% |
| Nepean | -1.3% |
| West Gate | +3.1% |

Four of the five monitored corridors recorded lower traffic volumes than their matched March baseline.

The strongest and most consistent reduction occurred on the Tullamarine corridor.

## Interpretation

The results suggest free public transport changed travel behaviour, but its effects on road traffic were modest and uneven.

This may reflect the fact that travel behaviour does not change instantly. Many people may try public transport when fares are removed, but long-term mode shift depends on service quality, reliability, frequency, accessibility and personal circumstances.

## What comes next

Victoria has now transitioned from free public transport to 50% discounted fares for the remainder of 2026.

Further monitoring will examine whether behavioural changes observed during the free fare period persist during the discounted fare period.
