# Methodology

## Overview

This project uses a mixed-methods and multi-source research design to evaluate travel behaviour responses to Victoria's 2026 free and discounted public transport policy.

The analysis combines:

- Behavioural survey data.
- Traffic monitoring data.
- Public transport operational data.
- Future planned analysis of aggregated mobility data, subject to data access approval.

## Survey

A survey of 1,028 Victorian residents was conducted during the free public transport period.

The survey examined:

- Public transport use.
- Car use.
- Car-to-public-transport substitution.
- Trip purposes.
- Barriers to public transport use.
- Future travel intentions.
- Support for affordable public transport during cost-of-living pressure.

Only aggregate survey results are reported publicly.

## Traffic analysis

Traffic was analysed across five monitored Melbourne corridors:

- Eastern Freeway
- Monash Freeway
- Nepean Highway corridor
- Tullamarine Freeway
- West Gate corridor

Traffic data were sourced from publicly available SCATS and TIRTL datasets.

## Baseline

Traffic during the free public transport period was compared with matched March 2026 baseline conditions.

The harmonised analysis used:

- March-only pre-policy baseline.
- Weekday-matched comparisons.
- Active detector/site coverage checks.
- Volume per active monitoring site.
- Separate treatment of Easter and school holiday periods.
