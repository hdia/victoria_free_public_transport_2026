# Traffic Change by Policy Period

## Corridor Performance Across Different Periods

| Corridor    | Launch Period | Easter Period | School Holidays (excluding Easter) | Post-Holiday Normal Period |
| ----------- | ------------: | ------------: | ---------------------------------: | -------------------------: |
| Eastern     |         +0.1% |        -30.2% |                              -9.9% |                      +0.0% |
| Monash      |         +3.3% |        -21.8% |                              -4.6% |                      -3.1% |
| Nepean      |         -1.3% |        -29.0% |                              -5.4% |                      -0.0% |
| Tullamarine |         -0.4% |        -22.5% |                              -4.8% |                      -7.2% |
| West Gate   |         -6.8% |        -28.4% |                             -17.3% |                      +9.9% |

## Interpretation

Traffic reductions were largest during Easter and school holiday periods.

When these periods are excluded, more modest but still measurable reductions remain on most monitored corridors, particularly Tullamarine and Monash.

This finding highlights the importance of controlling for seasonal and holiday effects when evaluating transport policies.
